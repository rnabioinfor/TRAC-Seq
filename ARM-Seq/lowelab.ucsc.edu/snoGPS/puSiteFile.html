What is a Pseudouridine File?

Click here for an example of a pseudouridine file based on the spliceosomal pseudouridine sites in H. sapiens.

Note that each line of the file consists of three items separated by white space: the sequence name (which **must** correspond to those in the target files that are pasted in or uploaded), a one-word comment, and the pseudouridine site numbers (separated by colons).
